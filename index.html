IGSHPA

International Ground Source Heat Pump Association of Canada.

The Canadian Chapter of the International Ground Source Heat Pump Association was created to support practitioners in the ground-coupled heat pump industry, to promote the industry to different levels of government, to provide training for system designers and builders, and to provide information to residential and commercial owners about the multiple benefits of the technology. A key objective of IGSHPA Canada is to explain that each system produces far more renewable energy than it consumes, and is classified as a NetZeroPlus technology.

Technology

Ground source heat pumps (GSHPs) are electrically powered systems that tap the stored energy of the greatest solar collector in existence: the earth.

It's one of the most efficient means available in Canada to provide space heating / cooling for homes and offices. It transfers the heat located immediately under the earth's surface (or in a body of water) into a building in winter, using the same principle as a refrigerator that extracts heat from food and rejects into a kitchen. A heat pump takes heat from its source at low temperature and discharges it at a higher temperature, allowing the unit to supply more heat than the equivalent energy supplied to the heat pump.
